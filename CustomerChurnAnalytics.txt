1) PROBLEM STATEMENT 


Project overview 
- This project builds a production-ready churn prediction system for a telecom business. A FastAPI service hosts a trained churn model and a Streamlit dashboard collects customer attributes, sends them to the API, and displays churn probability plus business recommendations. The goal is to turn raw subscription and service data into a practical retention decision tool.

Problem statement (clear and specific)
- Given a customer's profile, services, and billing details, predict the probability that the customer will churn in the near future and recommend retention actions that are aligned to business cost and risk.

Why this matters (business framing)
- Telecom revenue is recurring, so churn directly impacts lifetime value.
- Retention interventions are cheaper than reacquisition, so accurate risk scoring reduces cost and improves ROI.
- The model enables proactive actions rather than reactive firefighting.

Real-world applications
- Targeted retention offers and discounts for high-risk customers.
- Priority support and outreach for customers showing dissatisfaction signals.
- Contract and pricing strategy optimization based on churn drivers.

3) DATA PREPROCESSING (WHAT, WHY, HOW)

Handling missing values
- TotalCharges can be blank for new customers. Fix by coercing to numeric and imputing 0 or MonthlyCharges * tenure.
- For categorical values, fill with "No" or "Unknown" to keep schema stable.

Encoding categorical variables
- One-hot encode multi-class fields (Contract, PaymentMethod, InternetService).
- Map Yes/No features to binary for interpretability.

Feature scaling
- Standardize numeric columns for linear models; trees are less sensitive.

Outlier detection
- Check for extreme MonthlyCharges/TotalCharges; cap by percentile if needed.

Data cleaning
- Remove customerID from modeling features.
- Normalize category text values to match training vocabulary.

5) FEATURE ENGINEERING (FROM src/features.py)

Tenure features
- tenure_months = tenure
- tenure_years = tenure_months / 12

Monetary features
- monthly_charges = MonthlyCharges
- total_charges = TotalCharges
- avg_monthly_charges = total_charges / (tenure_months + 1)

RFM-style proxies (adapted to telecom)
- recency = max_tenure - tenure_months
- frequency = count of "Yes" across service columns
- monetary = total_charges

Engagement score
- engagement_score = frequency * 2 + tenure_months / 6

Complaint proxy
- complaint_proxy = (TechSupport == "Yes") + (OnlineSecurity == "No") + (DeviceProtection == "No")

Why these features
- They capture customer value, product adoption, and service quality signals that correlate with churn.

6) MODEL SELECTION

Baseline
- Logistic Regression for interpretability and quick sanity check.

Stronger models
- Random Forest, Gradient Boosting to capture non-linear patterns.

Why these models
- Telecom churn is tabular with mixed categorical/numeric features.
- Tree-based models handle interactions without manual feature crosses.

Model-by-model explanation and trade-offs
- Logistic Regression
	* Pros: fast, interpretable coefficients, easy to explain in interviews.
	* Cons: limited non-linear modeling, requires good feature engineering.
- Random Forest
	* Pros: captures non-linear patterns, robust to noise, handles interactions.
	* Cons: less interpretable, larger model size.
- Gradient Boosting
	* Pros: strong accuracy on tabular data, good bias-variance trade-off.
	* Cons: sensitive to hyperparameters, slower to train.

Why not deep learning here
- Dataset size is modest; tabular data is often best served by tree-based models rather than deep nets.
- Goal is explainability and stable inference in production.

7) TRAINING PROCESS

Train-test split / CV
- Stratified split (80/20) to preserve churn ratio.
- Cross-validation to avoid overfitting to a single split.

Hyperparameter tuning
- GridSearch/RandomizedSearch over tree depth, number of estimators, min samples per leaf, and regularization strength.

Overfitting control
- Regularization, depth limits, and validation curves.

8) EVALUATION METRICS

Metrics used
- Primary: ROC-AUC, Recall, F1 (churn class)
- Secondary: Precision, Accuracy

Why these metrics
- Accuracy is misleading with class imbalance.
- Recall reduces missed churners (high cost).
- ROC-AUC evaluates ranking quality across thresholds.

Confusion matrix focus
- False Negatives are most costly (missed churners).
- False Positives are acceptable if retention cost is lower than churn loss.

15) FINANCIAL AND BUSINESS TERMS 
Churn: Customer cancels service.
Retention: Keeping existing customers active.
Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): Total expected revenue from a customer.
Recurring Revenue: Ongoing revenue from subscriptions.
Acquisition Cost (CAC): Cost to acquire a new customer.
Retention ROI: Return on money spent to retain customers.
Discount Leakage: Lost revenue from giving discounts to low-risk customers.
Upsell: Encourage customer to buy higher tier services.
Cross-sell: Offer additional services (e.g., streaming add-ons).
Margin: Revenue minus cost of service.
Risk Score: Model probability of churn.
Threshold: Probability cutoff to label churn vs non-churn.

16) FULL EXPLANATION OF TERMS, FEATURES, CONCEPTS, THEORIES

Tenure
- Months a customer has been with the company. Low tenure often indicates low loyalty and higher churn risk.

MonthlyCharges
- Customer's recurring bill. High charges can increase price sensitivity.

TotalCharges
- Cumulative spend; proxy for customer value and engagement.

Contract type
- Longer contracts indicate commitment; month-to-month is flexible and higher risk.

Payment method
- Electronic check users often show higher churn due to lower friction to leave.

RFM-style features
- Recency: how recent the engagement is (here, shorter tenure implies higher recency)
- Frequency: number of services used, proxy for product adoption
- Monetary: total spend

Engagement score
- Combines service adoption and tenure to quantify product stickiness.

Complaint proxy
- Proxy for dissatisfaction using missing security/support services.

Classification threshold
- Transforms probability into label. Business can tune it to balance cost of false positives vs false negatives.

ROC-AUC
- Measures ranking performance across thresholds; higher is better.

Recall
- Fraction of churners correctly identified. High recall reduces missed churn.

Precision
- Fraction of predicted churners that truly churn. Controls retention cost.

17) INTERVIEW FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS YOU SHOULD EXPECT

Q1: Why did you choose rule-based insights instead of SHAP?
- This project prioritizes simple, stable, and fast production behavior.
- Rule-based insights are deterministic, low-latency, and easy to explain to non-technical stakeholders.
- SHAP can be added offline or on-demand later; it is heavier at runtime and requires careful governance for consistency.

Q2: How do you handle unseen categories in production?
- The API aligns incoming columns to the model preprocessor schema and fills missing or unknown categories with safe defaults.
- Training uses OneHotEncoder with a fixed category list; unseen values are treated as "other" (or dropped) by the encoder configuration.
- This prevents runtime failures and keeps the pipeline stable.

Q3: What threshold would you use if retention costs are high?
- Increase the threshold to reduce false positives and avoid wasting spend.
- Choose a threshold that minimizes expected cost, e.g., cost(FP) vs cost(FN).
- Calibrate this using a validation set and a business cost matrix.

Q4: How would you monitor drift and model degradation?
- Track input feature distributions and compare to training baselines.
- Monitor performance on delayed labels (AUC, recall) and business KPIs.
- Set alerts for significant shifts and retrain if drift exceeds thresholds.

Q5: How do you justify recall vs precision trade-offs?
- Missing churners (low recall) usually costs more than contacting a few low-risk customers (precision loss), but it depends on retention cost.
- Use a cost matrix and choose the threshold that minimizes total loss.
- Align the trade-off with business budget and campaign capacity.

Q6: Why not use deep learning?
- Dataset size is modest and tabular; tree models are typically stronger and more interpretable here.

Q7: How do you ensure training and inference consistency?
- Use a single preprocessing pipeline saved with the model and apply feature engineering in the API using the same logic as training.

Q8: How would you explain a single prediction to a stakeholder?
- Show churn probability, top drivers (tenure, contract, support services), and the actionable insight generated.

Q9: What is the biggest risk in production?
- Category drift or input schema changes; mitigated by validation, defaults, and monitoring.

Q10: How do you validate the business impact?
- Run a controlled A/B test on retention campaigns and measure lift in churn reduction or revenue retained.
